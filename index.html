" The State, founded on the consent of the governed, is an instrument serving the common good and the preservation of civil society. Its mission is to promote justice, facilitate the development of prosperity and ensure peace and security for all its citizens "

- John Locke -

In an era of rapid change, both in societal dynamics and governance structures, the concept of the nation-state has come under increasing scrutiny. While extensive research has shed light on its complexities, the notion itself has become somewhat nebulous. Recognizing this evolving landscape, there is a growing interest in distilling the essence of the state. As such, we propose exploring the foundational principles outlined in the Montevideo Convention. This framework offers individuals the opportunity to create and assert sovereignty over their own state, reflecting the diverse and evolving nature of governance in our modern world.
